Resume Building

Step 1: Gather Your Information

Before you start, prepare details for the following sections:

Personal Information

  • Full Name
  • Email (Use a professional one)
  • Phone Number
  • LinkedIn, GitHub, Portfolio (Ensure all links are working)

Education

  • Degree, University, Graduation Year
  • Relevant coursework (if applicable)

Work Experience

  • Internships, part-time jobs, research experience
  • Company name, position, duration
  • Key achievements (Use action verbs and quantify impact)

Projects

  • Project name, technologies used
  • Key contributions (Explain what you did clearly)

Skills

  • Programming languages, frameworks, tools
  • Soft skills (only if relevant)

Certifications & Awards (Optional)

  • Relevant online courses, hackathons, competitions

Extracurricular Activities (Optional)

  • Leadership roles, volunteering, clubs

Step 2: Set Up Overleaf

Go to Overleaf and create a free account.

Click “New Project” and choose “Resume” from templates.

If using a template, select one suitable for your field (modern, ATS-friendly, etc.).

You can refer to the following resume templates for guidance:

  • Resume Format 1
  • Resume Format 2
  • DTU Sample Resume

Step 3: Understanding LaTeX Basics for Resumes

LaTeX uses a structured syntax for document formatting. Below is a simple resume template in LaTeX:

        \documentclass[a4paper,10pt]{article}
        \usepackage{geometry}
        \geometry{margin=1in}
        \usepackage{enumitem}

        \begin{document}

        \begin{center}
            {\LARGE \textbf{Your Name}} \\
            Email | Phone | LinkedIn | GitHub
        \end{center}

        \section*{Education}
        \textbf{Degree}, University – Year of Graduation

        \section*{Experience}
        \textbf{Internship Title}, Company (Duration)
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Key responsibility or achievement
            \item Key responsibility or achievement
        \end{itemize}

        \section*{Projects}
        \textbf{Project Name} (Tech Stack)
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Short description of your contribution
        \end{itemize}

        \section*{Skills}
        Python, Django, React, Machine Learning, Cybersecurity, etc.

        \end{document}

Step 4: Customize and Format Your Resume

  • Use Bold Text for company names and job roles.
  • Keep Bullet Points Concise and action-oriented.
  • Maintain Readability by adjusting font size and margins if necessary.
  • Stick to a One-Page Format unless you have extensive experience.

Step 5: Export and Use Your Resume

  • Click “Recompile” in Overleaf to preview formatting.
  • Download the PDF once satisfied.
  • Ensure all links are functional before submitting applications.

Pro Tips for an Effective Resume

  • ✅ Use Action Verbs – Designed, Developed, Led, Implemented, Optimized, etc.
  • ✅ Quantify Impact – “Increased efficiency by 20%” sounds stronger than “Improved efficiency.”
  • ✅ Tailor to the Job – Customize keywords based on the job description.
  • ✅ Keep It Simple – No need for fancy colors or graphics; readability is key.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Code for Good Hackathon?

The Code for Good Hackathon by JPMorgan Chase & Co. (JPMC) is a corporate event aimed at encouraging students to solve real-world problems using technology. The event not only allows participants to showcase their technical and teamwork skills but also introduces them to JPMC's culture and technology infrastructure.

  • Participants are grouped into teams, usually consisting of 5-7 members.
  • Teams work on problems presented by nonprofit organizations associated with JPMC.
  • Problems focus on social good, addressing challenges faced by nonprofits (NGOs).
  • Examples include creating platforms for fundraising, optimizing resource distribution, or improving accessibility.
  • JPMC employees mentor teams, offering guidance on technical implementation and best practices.
  • The hackathon doubles as a recruitment event, where participants can showcase their skills to JPMC recruiters.

Selection Process

  1. 8.5 and above CGPA
  2. Online DSA round - 2 questions (medium to hard; topics include graphs, arrays, strings, stacks, etc.)
  3. Online HireVue Interview round - HR interview with questions like:
    • What skills will you showcase while working in a team?
    • Share an experience where you showcased your problem-solving skills?

The Hackathon

  • If selected, you will receive an invite from the CODE FOR GOOD team, which you must accept.
  • The hackathon was conducted offline in 2024 but was online prior to this.
  • Make travel arrangements for the hackathon as soon as possible. The travel money is reimbursed, so keep a record of all receipts and tickets.
  • Prepare well for the hackathon. Some resources are shared below.
  • All students selected for the hackathon are divided into teams of 7-8 members each. Each team consists of participants from different colleges. There could be a language barrier among your teammates, so work on your communication skills and spoken English as well.
  • Each team has two mentors who are actual JPMC employees. Interact with them nicely. The mentors can also take your personal interview.

Getting selected for SEP Summer Internship

  • It is not necessary that only the winners of the hackathon will get the internship opportunity.
  • You get selected based on your performance in the hackathon, so do your best.
  • Some participants got the selection mail one month after the hackathon, whereas some got it even after 2-2.5 months.
